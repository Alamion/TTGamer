# Dark Side Resistance

**Dark Side Resistance (DSR)** measures how well your character resists the temptation of the dark side of the Force. For all practical purposes, treat it like Humanity in _Vampire: The Masquerade_ — every time a character commits a heinous act, the Storyteller calls for a Conscience roll to see if their resistance is reduced.

Starting DSR equals **5 + Conscience – Passion**. It ranges from **1** (minimum: Conscience 1, Passion 5) to **9** (maximum: Conscience 5, Passion 1).

---

## The Formula

```
Dark Side Resistance = 5 + Conscience − Passion
```

A character with high Conscience and low Passion resists the dark side well — they are moral and not easily swayed by emotion. A character with low Conscience and high Passion is already close to the edge.

**Example:** Jax Vorn has Conscience 3 and Passion 2. His DSR is 5 + 3 − 2 = **6**. He has a decent moral compass and moderate emotional intensity — he can be tempted but has a strong foundation.

---

## How Degeneration Works

When a character commits a morally questionable act, the Storyteller decides whether a Conscience roll is required. If so:

1. Roll **Conscience** (difficulty determined by the severity of the act — see [Hierarchy](#hierarchy-of-sins) below).
2. **Success** — The character maintains their current DSR. They feel guilt, acknowledge the wrong, but hold onto their resistance.
3. **Failure** — The character loses **1 point** of Dark Side Resistance.

The difficulty of the Conscience roll depends on the character's current DSR level. A character with high DSR must roll for lesser sins, while a character with low DSR only risks degeneration for truly heinous acts.

### Hierarchy of Sins

| Current DSR | Minimum Wrongdoing for Conscience Roll  |
| ----------- | --------------------------------------- |
| 9–10        | Selfish thoughts or petty cruelty       |
| 7–8         | Purposefully inflicting minor harm      |
| 5–6         | Theft, robbery, or negligent harm       |
| 3–4         | Murder in the heat of passion           |
| 1–2         | Premeditated murder, torture, or sadism |
| 0           | The most heinous and demented acts      |

## Automatic DSR Loss (Force Users)

Force-using characters are particularly susceptible to the dark side. The original WEG _Star Wars Roleplaying Game_ lists certain acts that result in automatically gaining Dark Side Points. In this conversion, instead of gaining a Dark Side Point, the character **automatically loses 1 point of DSR** — no Conscience roll.

These acts include:

- **Killing a helpless or defenseless opponent** in anger
- **Using a Force Power to cause unnecessary suffering** or death
- **Deliberately harming an innocent** for personal gain
- **Breaking a sworn oath** to a Master or the Jedi Order
- **Using the "Release Your Anger" option** to temporarily boost a Force Skill

**"Release Your Anger" (Optional Rule):** A Force user may temporarily substitute their Passion for a Force Skill for one scene. This requires no roll — they simply give in to anger and let the hate flow through them. But the path to the dark side is quick and seductive: they **automatically lose 1 point of DSR** when they do this.

---

## What Happens at 0 DSR?

Unlike Humanity in V:TM, a character who loses all Dark Side Resistance can **still remain a playable character**. The expanded universe has shown Sith and other dark side Force users as protagonists, and Storytellers should have the option to explore this.

A character at 0 DSR:

- Has fully embraced the dark side
- No longer rolls Conscience to avoid degeneration — they are beyond guilt
- May gain access to alternative mechanics (see **Vanquish** and Dark Side Force Powers)
- May face **social ostracism** (see Optional Rule below)

**Optional Rule — Social Ostracism:** As a character gives in to anger, fear, and aggression, they begin to lose their emotional connection to other beings. Interpersonal relationships become more difficult. Other beings, even on first impression, have an innate fear and suspicion of anyone with so little compassion. Some dark side characters with high Social Attributes may overcome this, but most will find themselves increasingly distrusted. The Storyteller should make sure this is felt by the player.

---

## Regaining Dark Side Resistance

Once lost, DSR is difficult to recover.

### With Experience Points

The cost to buy Dark Side Resistance with Experience Points is **10 minus current level**.

| Current DSR | XP Cost for Next Dot |
| ----------- | -------------------- |
| 0           | 10 XP                |
| 1           | 9 XP                 |
| 2           | 8 XP                 |
| 3           | 7 XP                 |
| 4           | 6 XP                 |
| 5           | 5 XP                 |
| 6           | 4 XP                 |
| 7           | 3 XP                 |
| 8           | 2 XP                 |

This is not an investment most players would be willing to make without strong motivation.

### Through Roleplay

Storytellers may allow players to earn back DSR through sustained roleplay — genuine remorse, selfless acts, seeking redemption. This should **never be quick or easy**. A redeemed character's journey should be the subject of an entire story arc, if not a whole chronicle.

### At Character Creation

DSR can be raised independently with **Freebie Points** at a cost of **1 point per dot**.

---

## Droids and DSR

Droids cannot learn Force Skills, but they **may still spend and regain Force Points and lose Dark Side Resistance**. A droid that commits atrocities can fall to the dark side just as a living being can.

---

## Non-Force User Campaigns

If your campaign has no Force-using characters, you may **ignore DSR entirely**. Set starting Willpower to 6 and starting Force Points to 3. See [Virtues & Willpower](./virtues-willpower.mdx).
