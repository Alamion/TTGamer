## Step 1: Concept, Species & Home World

Every memorable hero begins with an idea. Your **Concept** is a short phrase that captures who your character is — their background, profession, and defining traits rolled into one.

### Concept

Your concept answers: _What did you do before the adventure started?_ and _How do you approach problems?_ Think of it as your elevator pitch:

- _Corellian Smuggler with a heart of gold_
- _Disgraced Jedi Padawan on the run_
- _Rebel Commando turned bounty hunter_
- _Loyal Imperial officer with a crisis of conscience_

The character templates in the back of the Star Wars Roleplaying Game source books make excellent starting Concepts.

### Nature & Demeanor

**Nature** is your character's true inner self — what they are deep down when the masks come off. **Demeanor** is the face they show the galaxy. These can be identical (a straightforward character) or very different (a manipulative character who plays the fool).

Choose from the standard V:TM archetypes:

| Archetype          | Nature (True Self)                         | Demeanor (Public Face)                               |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Autocrat**       | Must be in control                         | Commands respect through confidence or fear          |
| **Bon Vivant**     | Lives for pleasure and sensation           | Charismatic, fun-loving, never serious               |
| **Bravo**          | Solves everything through force            | Aggressive, confrontational, quick to escalate       |
| **Caregiver**      | Protects and nurtures others               | Warm, approachable, always helping                   |
| **Conniver**       | Manipulates others for gain                | Charming, agreeable, hard to pin down                |
| **Conformist**     | Needs to belong                            | Blends in, follows the group, avoids standing out    |
| **Curmudgeon**     | Never satisfied, always complaining        | Grouchy, difficult, secretly dependable              |
| **Deviant**        | Different, outcast, proud of it            | Eccentric, unpredictable, hard to read               |
| **Director**       | Orchestrates events and people             | Calm, persuasive, always suggesting "good ideas"     |
| **Fanatic**        | Devoted to a cause above all               | Intense, focused, hard to distract                   |
| **Gallant**        | Wants to be admired                        | Showy, dramatic, loves the spotlight                 |
| **Judge**          | Evaluates and pronounces                   | Measured, deliberate, few words                      |
| **Loner**          | Works best alone                           | Distant, self-sufficient, hard to trust              |
| **Martyr**         | Seeks suffering for a cause                | Self-sacrificing, quietly heroic                     |
| **Masochist**      | Seeks pain (physical or emotional)         | Invites conflict, pushes buttons                     |
| **Monster**        | Has surrendered to the dark side           | Cruel, sadistic, or simply empty                     |
| **Pedagogue**      | Must teach and guide others                | Patient, instructive, philosophical                  |
| **Penitent**       | Seeks redemption for past sins             | Humble, apologetic, eager to prove themselves        |
| **Perfectionist**  | Nothing is ever good enough                | Meticulous, critical, hard on themselves and others  |
| **Rebel**          | Defies authority on principle              | Antagonistic, refuses orders, questions everything   |
| **Rogue**          | Lives by their own code                    | Charming but unreliable, principled in their own way |
| **Schemer**        | Always planning the next move              | Calculating, observant, plays the long game          |
| **Survivor**       | Does whatever it takes to live another day | Pragmatic, adaptable, hard to corner                 |
| **Thrill-Seeker**  | Lives for the adrenaline rush              | Reckless, bold, always takes the risk                |
| **Traditionalist** | Upholds the old ways                       | Conservative, formal, suspicious of change           |
| **Visionary**      | Sees what could be, not what is            | Inspiring, dreamy, sometimes impractical             |

**Example:** _Jax Vorn_, the Corellian smuggler introduced in the Quick Start, has **Nature: Rogue** (he lives by his own code, not the Empire's) and **Demeanor: Gallant** (he talks a big game, flashes that smile, and loves being the center of attention — even if he's not as confident as he seems).

### Species

The galaxy teems with diverse sentient species. Your species determines your physical appearance, cultural background, and any physiological merits or flaws that set you apart from the human baseline.

**Humans** are the most numerous and politically dominant species, found in every corner of the galaxy — from the Core Worlds to the Outer Rim. Their adaptability makes them a solid choice for any concept.

**Twi'leks** (from Ryloth) are known for their head-tails (lekku) and colorful skin. They excel as diplomats, spies, and information brokers thanks to their natural charisma and linguistic talent.

**Wookiees** (from Kashyyyk) are towering, strong-willed warriors with a fierce sense of honor. Their strength and durability make them formidable in combat, though their temper can be a liability.

**Rodians** (from Rodia) have a reputation as bounty hunters and mercenaries. Their reptilian senses grant them keen perception and tracking instincts.

**Duros** (from Duros) are a ancient spacefaring species with large eyes and blue-green skin. They are natural pilots and navigators, their civilization having charted the hyperlanes millennia ago.

**Bothans** (from Bothawui) are famous (or infamous) for their intricate spy networks and political maneuvering. Their curiosity and cunning make them excellent information gatherers.

**Mon Calamari** (from Mon Cala) are an amphibious species known for their artistic sensibility and technological innovation — especially starship design. Their cousins, the **Quarren**, are more pragmatic and isolationist.

**Zabrak** (from Iridonia) are a proud, independent species identifiable by their facial horns and tribal tattoos. Their strong will and tolerance for pain make them natural survivors.

**Togruta** (from Shili) have montrals and head-tails that grant them heightened spatial awareness. They are often drawn to artistic or spiritual pursuits — many Jedi originate from this species.

**Kel Dor** (from Dorin) must wear protective goggles and breath masks outside their homeworld's helium-rich atmosphere. They are known for their keen intellect and stoic demeanor.

Select your species from those available in your campaign — the [Species](../species.mdx) catalog includes era-tagged variants. Consult your Storyteller about which species fit the setting.

### Home World

Your character's home world shapes their perspective, skills, and outlook as much as their species. Consider where they grew up and how that environment forged them:

| Home World    | Region      | Character                           |
| ------------- | ----------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Coruscant** | Core Worlds | Urban, political, fast-paced        |
| **Naboo**     | Mid Rim     | Refined, artistic, peaceful         |
| **Alderaan**  | Core Worlds | Cultured, diplomatic, pacifist      |
| **Corellia**  | Core Worlds | Independent, mechanical, smuggler's |
| **Tatooine**  | Outer Rim   | Harsh, survivalist, no-nonsense     |
| **Kashyyyk**  | Mytaranor   | Wookiee home, arboreal, clan-bound  |
| **Ryloth**    | Outer Rim   | Twi'lek home, rugged, resourceful   |
| **Dathomir**  | Outer Rim   | Wild, untamed, strong in the Force  |
| **Mandalore** | Outer Rim   | Warrior culture, honor-bound        |
| **Bespin**    | Outer Rim   | Cloud cities, mining, cosmopolitan  |
| **Lothal**    | Outer Rim   | Rural, agricultural, pastoral       |
| **Nal Hutta** | Outer Rim   | Hutt space, criminal, decadent      |

A character from Coruscant's lower levels will have very different instincts than one raised on a peaceful Naboo lake country estate. Discuss with your Storyteller how your home world influences your starting skills and roleplaying opportunities.

Each species may also adjust your starting **Freebie Points** based on biological Merits and Flaws. A species with net advantages costs more; a species with disadvantages grants bonus points.
