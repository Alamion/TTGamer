## Step 6: Merits & Flaws

**Merits** are special advantages that set your character apart — natural talent, unique training, cybernetics, or fortunate circumstances. **Flaws** are the burdens they carry — enemies, obligations, physical drawbacks, or psychological weaknesses.

### How Merits & Flaws Work

Every character starts with **0 points** of Merits or Flaws. You may purchase Merits using Freebie Points (spent in Step 7), and you may take Flaws to gain additional Freebie Points.

| Rule                      | Detail                                                      |
| ------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Merit cost**            | A `+3` Merit costs **3 Freebie Points**                     |
| **Flaw rebate**           | A `-3` Flaw grants **3 bonus Freebie Points**               |
| **Net limit at creation** | Merits minus Flaws cannot exceed **7 points**               |
| **Alien Physiology**      | Species-specific traits are marked as **Alien Physiology**  |
| **Force-related Merits**  | Required to access Force Skills (e.g., **Force Sensitive**) |

### Species Physiology

Non-human species often include biological Merits and Flaws as part of their template. For example, a Wookiee may have **Natural Weaponry (Claws)** as a merit and **Short Temper** as a flaw. These are factored into the species' Freebie Point adjustment.

### Choosing Your Merits & Flaws

When selecting Merits and Flaws, think about your character's backstory:

- **Combat-oriented:** Daredevil, Iron Will, Natural Weaponry
- **Social-oriented:** Allies, Fame, Ambidextrous
- **Force-oriented:** Force Sensitive, Strong in the Force, Natural Force Power
- **Burdens:** Enemy, Dark Side Susceptibility, Code of Honor, Phobia
