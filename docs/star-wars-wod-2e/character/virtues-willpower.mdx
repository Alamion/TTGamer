# Virtues & Willpower

In the Star Wars universe, the Force is tied closely to emotion — "Search your feelings", "Release your anger", "When you are calm, at peace, passive". The **Virtues** of this system replace the World of Darkness Courage virtue with **Passion**, adapting the V:TM moral framework to model a character's emotional relationship with the Force.

Every character — not just Force users — has three Virtues rated 1–5, with **7 dots** to allocate across them at character creation.

---

## The Three Virtues

### Conscience

Conscience is your character's innate sense of right and wrong. It represents their moral compass, empathy, and ability to feel guilt.

**Conscience is the Virtue used to avoid losing Dark Side Resistance.** When a character commits a heinous act (murder, torture, betrayal), the Storyteller calls for a Conscience roll — success means they maintain their resistance; failure means they lose a point of Dark Side Resistance.

Even characters with no connection to the Force benefit from maintaining a healthy Conscience. An optional rule in [Dark Side Resistance](./dark_side_resistance.mdx) explains why.

| Dots  | Description                                                                                      |
| ----- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| •     | Flexible morals. You do what you need to survive, and sleep fine afterward.                      |
| ••    | A basic sense of right and wrong, but you can rationalize most things.                           |
| •••   | You feel genuine remorse for wrong actions. You try to make amends when you can.                 |
| ••••  | Your conscience is loud and insistent. Wrongdoing haunts you, and you actively seek to avoid it. |
| ••••• | You are guided by an unshakeable moral code. Your integrity is legendary.                        |

**High Conscience:** Yoda, Padmé Amidala
**Low Conscience:** Jabba the Hutt, Darth Maul

---

### Passion

Passion is a measure of your character's capacity to feel emotion — love and hate, joy and anger, courage and fear. In V:TM this virtue was **Courage**; in this conversion it has been replaced to better reflect the emotional core of the Star Wars universe.

**Passion serves two mechanical roles:**

1. **Willpower** — Starting Willpower equals Passion + Self-Control.
2. **Force Point bonus** — When you spend a Force Point, instead of taking one automatic success, you may add a number of dice to your roll equal to your Passion. This is the mechanical equivalent of "releasing your anger" or "using your hate".

For non-Force users, Passion represents the intensity of effort you can put forth under extreme stress — the surge of adrenaline that lets a desperate parent leap a chasm or a cornered pilot pull off an impossible maneuver.

| Dots  | Description                                                                      |
| ----- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| •     | Calm, detached, hard to rattle. Others may see you as cold or unfeeling.         |
| ••    | You feel emotions deeply but keep them in check. You are steady, not passionate. |
| •••   | You feel strongly and express it. Your emotions are evident to those around you. |
| ••••  | Your emotions burn hot. You love fiercely, hate powerfully, and act on feeling.  |
| ••••• | You are a creature of pure emotion — passionate, volatile, and driven.           |

**High Passion:** Han Solo, Kylo Ren
**Low Passion:** Chirrut Îmwe, Yoda

---

### Self-Control

Self-Control reflects your character's patience, resolve, and focus. It represents the ability to remain calm under pressure, resist temptation, and persist through frustration.

**Self-Control determines two derived stats:**

1. **Willpower** — Starting Willpower equals Passion + Self-Control.
2. **Force Points** — Starting Force Points equal Self-Control.

**Optional Rules:**

- When a failed roll can be re-rolled, characters are limited to a number of re-rolls equal to their Self-Control before they become too frustrated to continue.
- For tedious tasks with no guaranteed payoff (stake-outs, searching archives, waiting for a contact), the Storyteller may call for Self-Control rolls. The player with the most successes has the patience to stick with it.

| Dots  | Description                                                                        |
| ----- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| •     | Impulsive and easily frustrated. Patience is not your virtue.                      |
| ••    | You have some self-discipline but prefer quick results.                            |
| •••   | You can focus through distractions and persist through setbacks.                   |
| ••••  | You are remarkably composed. It takes a lot to shake you.                          |
| ••••• | Your focus is unbreakable. You can wait, endure, and persist beyond normal limits. |

**High Self-Control:** R2-D2, Ben Kenobi
**Low Self-Control:** Chewbacca, Kylo Ren

## Derived Stats

Once Virtues are allocated, calculate these derived values. They are **set at character creation** — raising a Virtue later does not retrigger these formulas, but each derived stat can be raised independently with Freebie or Experience points.

| Stat                     | Formula                  | Description                                                                                                              |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Willpower**            | Passion + Self-Control   | A static defense against mental intrusion, coercion, and Force powers. See below.                                        |
| **Force Points**         | Self-Control             | Spend to gain automatic successes or extra dice. Regained through roleplaying your Nature. See below.                    |
| **Dark Side Resistance** | 5 + Conscience – Passion | Your resistance to the dark side. Treated like Humanity in V:TM. See [Dark Side Resistance](./dark_side_resistance.mdx). |

---

## Willpower

Starting Willpower equals **Passion + Self-Control**. It ranges from 2 (minimum 1 each) to 10 (maximum 5 each).

**Willpower is a static trait in this conversion.** Unlike V:TM, Willpower points cannot be spent for automatic successes. Instead:

- Willpower is used to **determine difficulty** when others try to break your will, read your mind, or use Force powers against you.
- It represents your character's **individuality, stubborn determination, and general pig-headedness**.
- In opposed rolls against mental or Force-based attacks, the difficulty is typically equal to the target's Willpower.

**Raising Willpower:** Costs 2 Freebie Points per dot at character creation, or 2 Experience Points per dot during play.

All other functions of Willpower from V:TM — spending for automatic successes, regaining through roleplay — have been taken over by **Force Points**.

---

## Force Points

Starting Force Points equal **Self-Control**. Force Points range from 1 (minimum Self-Control) to 5 (maximum Self-Control).

**Force Points behave like Willpower in V:TM.** When a character absolutely must succeed, they may call on the Force to aid them — often unconsciously, simply trying as hard as they can.

### Spending a Force Point

A player has two options when spending a Force Point:

1. **Automatic success** — Gain one automatic success on a single roll.
2. **Passion dice** — Add a number of dice to your dice pool equal to your **Passion** virtue. You must decide before rolling.

**Example:** Rax needs to jump a collapsed bridge to save his son. He has Strength 2 and Athletics 1, but the gap is 15 meters — he needs four successes. He spends a Force Point and, instead of taking one automatic success, opts to add his Passion of 4 to the roll — rolling 7 dice (2+1+4) instead of 3.

### Regaining Force Points

Spent Force Points are regained by **roleplaying your character's Nature**.

The Nature archetype table (see [Creation Steps](./creation-steps/step-1-concept-species)) describes the condition under which each Nature regains Force Points. When you act in a way that genuinely fulfills your Nature — not just going through the motions to regenerate points — the Storyteller awards **1–3 Force Points**.

| Nature             | Regain Condition                                                             |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Autocrat**       | When you lead a group and accomplish something significant                   |
| **Bon Vivant**     | When you have a truly good time and express your exultation                  |
| **Bravo**          | When you intimidate or physically force someone to do what you want          |
| **Caregiver**      | When you successfully protect or nurture someone                             |
| **Conniver**       | When you trick someone into doing what you want                              |
| **Conformist**     | When your group accomplishes something because of your support               |
| **Curmudgeon**     | When someone does something stupid, just like you predicted                  |
| **Deviant**        | When you defy society's precepts without retaliation                         |
| **Director**       | When you organize and lead others to achieve a goal                          |
| **Fanatic**        | When you accomplish an act that furthers your cause                          |
| **Gallant**        | When you dazzle or impress another person                                    |
| **Judge**          | When you separate truth from lies or convince others to accept your judgment |
| **Loner**          | When you accomplish something significant on your own                        |
| **Martyr**         | When you sacrifice yourself for your beliefs or another                      |
| **Masochist**      | When you endure pain or humiliation                                          |
| **Monster**        | When you commit an act of cruelty or darkness                                |
| **Pedagogue**      | When you teach or guide someone to a breakthrough                            |
| **Penitent**       | When you make amends for a past wrong, no matter how small                   |
| **Perfectionist**  | When your meticulousness pays off in a tangible way                          |
| **Rebel**          | When your rebellion against the status quo turns out for the best            |
| **Rogue**          | When you get away with something by wit, charm, or luck                      |
| **Schemer**        | When one of your plans comes together                                        |
| **Survivor**       | When you survive a difficult situation through your own cunning              |
| **Thrill-Seeker**  | When you pull off a reckless, dangerous stunt                                |
| **Traditionalist** | When you protect the status quo and prevent change                           |
| **Visionary**      | When you convince others to believe in your vision                           |

### Raising Force Points

Costs 2 Freebie Points per dot at character creation, or 2 Experience Points per dot during play.

Force-using characters also use Force Points to **activate certain Force Powers**. See [Force](./force.mdx) for details.

---

## Optional: Games Without Force Users

If your campaign has no Force-using characters, you may **ignore Virtues entirely**. Set starting **Willpower** to 6 and starting **Force Points** to 3. Players may raise these with Freebie or Experience points later.
