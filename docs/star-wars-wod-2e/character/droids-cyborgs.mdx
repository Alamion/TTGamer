# Droids and Cyborgs

Computers think. Droids feel. The line blurs further when flesh is replaced with steel. This chapter covers the mechanical and narrative implications of playing a droid character or a cyborg — from creation rules and reprogramming to cybernetic enhancements and their costs.

---

## Droid Characters

Droids are not equipment — they are characters. They earn and spend Experience Points like living beings, and they follow the same creation process with these key differences:

- **Attribute Upgrades:** Droids do not spend XP on Attributes. Instead, their Attributes are upgraded like vehicle customizations. See the Upgrading Vehicles section for details. A droid's parts can also be sold off or stolen.
- **Reprogramming:** Droids can reallocate their Abilities during play without spending XP. See [Reprogramming](#reprogramming) below.
- **No Force Skills:** Droids cannot learn Force Skills. However, they may still spend and regain Force Points and lose Dark Side Resistance — a droid that commits atrocities can fall to the dark side just as a living being can.
- **Repair Instead of Healing:** Droids do not heal naturally. They recover Health levels through repair, following vehicle repair rules. They do not need stabilization and are immune to environmental hazards (heat, cold, poison).

### Player Character Droids

All droid player characters — regardless of degree, model, or function — are created with the **same starting dots** as any living character (7/5/3 Attributes, 13/9/5 Abilities, 7 Virtues). Use **Merits & Flaws** to reflect a droid's form and special capabilities.

At character creation, any equipment bought with Resources can be **built in** to the droid's chassis.

:::note[Arakyd Viper Probe Droid]
John builds a Probe Droid PC. After assigning Attributes and Abilities, he takes Resources and buys a Blaster Carbine, a Mine (self-destruct), and a Sensor Pack — all built into the chassis. He also buys a Comlink and uses Customization to boost its range. John takes **Flight Appendages** (repulsorlift movement), **Extra Appendages** (multiple manipulator arms), **Prehensile Appendages** (grasping with all arms), and **Ambidextrous**.
:::

### NPC Droid Degrees

When creating non-player character droids, use the following table to determine starting dots:

| Degree     | Attributes | Abilities | Backgrounds | Virtues |
| ---------- | ---------- | --------- | ----------- | ------- |
| **First**  | 7/5/3      | 13/9/5    | 0           | 7       |
| **Second** | 6/4/2      | 11/7/3    | 0           | 6       |
| **Third**  | 5/3/1      | 9/5/1     | 0           | 5       |
| **Fourth** | 4/2/0      | 7/3/0     | 0           | 4       |
| **Fifth**  | 3/2/0      | 5/1/0     | 0           | 3       |

## Droid Attributes

Rather than spending XP, a droid's Attributes are upgraded by installing better parts — faster processors, stronger servos, more sensitive sensors. This follows the **vehicle customization rules** (see the Vehicles chapter). The key implication is that a droid's Attributes can also be **sold or stolen**, making them both an asset and a liability.

---

## Droid Abilities & Memory

A droid's three Ability types — **Talents**, **Skills**, and **Knowledges** — each occupy different types of memory. This means dots can only be moved **within the same Ability type**. You cannot reallocate a Talent dot to a Knowledge.

| Memory Partition     | Ability Types                                                                                                                |
| -------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Talent Memory**    | Alertness, Athletics, Brawl, Dodge, Empathy, Expression, Intimidation, Leadership, Streetwise, Subterfuge                    |
| **Skill Memory**     | Animal Ken, Crafts, Drive, Etiquette, Firearms, Melee, Performance, Repair, Security, Stealth, Survival, Technology          |
| **Knowledge Memory** | Academics, Astrogation, Bureaucracy, Computer, Enigmas, Investigation, Law, Linguistics, Medicine, Occult, Politics, Science |

### Reprogramming

To reprogram a droid, the programmer must understand the Ability they are installing. If the programmer has 0 ranks in the new Ability, the Storyteller may require an **Investigation** roll to learn enough to write the program — difficulty and time are circumstantial.

For each dot the programmer wants to add, a dot from another Ability of the **same type** must first be deleted to free memory. The programmer then rolls **Intelligence + Programming**.

**Difficulty:** `4 + new dot level`

**Success Time Table:**

| Successes | Time Required |
| --------- | ------------- |
| 1         | 12 hours      |
| 2         | 8 hours       |
| 3         | 4 hours       |
| 4         | 2 hours       |
| 5+        | 1 hour        |

:::note[Protocol Droid to Medic]
Rax wants to retrain a protocol droid (Cultures 4, Languages 3, Interfaces 2) into a medic. He frees memory by deleting a dot of Languages, then rolls Intelligence + Programming at difficulty 5 (4 + new Medicine 1). Four successes: 2 hours. The droid now has Medicine 1, Languages 2. He then deletes a dot from Cultures and rolls difficulty 6 (4 + Medicine 2). Three successes: 4 hours. The droid now has Medicine 2, Cultures 3.
:::

**Failure:** The programmer has reached their limit for this task. They may try again when the situation changes — their skill increases, they get help, or they access better equipment.

**Botch:** The old dot is lost and the new dot is not gained. The droid can only regain the lost Ability dot by earning XP and spending it normally.

### Reprogramming Virtues and Demeanor

Virtues can be reprogrammed using the same rules as Abilities — roll Intelligence + Programming, difficulty `4 + new dot level`.

Reprogramming a droid's **Demeanor** is possible but difficult. Difficulty is **7**. On failure, the droid's Demeanor defaults to its Nature. On a botch, the Storyteller chooses a new Demeanor (personality Archetype) for the droid.

### Self-Reprogramming

There is no example of a droid reprogramming itself in the films. It is up to the Storyteller to decide whether a droid with the Programming skill can reprogram itself.

## Droid Damage & Recovery

Droids handle damage differently from living characters:

- **No natural healing** — Bashing and Lethal damage must be repaired
- **De-ionization** — Droids recover from Stun/ion damage at the same rate living beings recover from Stun
- **No stabilization needed** — A damaged droid will not continue losing Health levels regardless of severity
- **Environmental immunity** — Heat, cold, and poison have no effect
- **Permanent Repairs** — Droid recovery uses the Permanent Repairs rules from the Vehicle Encounters chapter
- **Botched Soak on Stun damage** — The droid reboots for `1d10` minutes instead of falling unconscious

### Ion Weapons

Ion weapons deal Stun damage to droids. Blasters set to stun have no effect on droids — ion technology is required.

---

## Force & Dark Side Resistance

Droids cannot learn Force Skills or purchase Force Powers. However, they may still spend Force Points, regain Force Points through roleplay, and lose Dark Side Resistance. A droid that commits heinous acts degrades its DSR exactly like a living character — see [Dark Side Resistance](./dark_side_resistance.mdx).

## Cybernetic Enhancements

Cybernetic characters are treated as **part-droid** for damage and recovery/repair. A cyborg can also take merits from the Alien Physiology list that they might not otherwise qualify for.

Cybernetic prosthetics must be custom-crafted for each recipient by a character with both **Tech** and **Medicine** knowledges. If acquired during play, cybernetics cost approximately **500 credits** each depending on availability.

### Cybernetic Limb (1-2 pt Merit)

One of your limbs has been replaced with a cybernetic prosthesis.

- **Partial limb** (hand, foot, below-knee) — 1 pt
- **Whole limb** (arm from shoulder, leg from hip) — 2 pt

Without additional customization, the limb functions identically to a natural limb, but damage to it is tracked separately from your Health Levels. You may take this merit multiple times for multiple limbs.

Assign each prosthesis one or two d10 numbers (depending on the merit value). When you take non-targeted damage, roll `1d10`. If the limb's number comes up, the limb takes the damage instead of you.

Damage to a prosthesis does not require stabilization and causes no additional Health loss. The wound penalty only applies to actions that involve the damaged limb.

:::note
Rax has a cybernetic left calf (1 pt, assigned `1`) and a cybernetic left arm (2 pt, assigned `2-3`). A bounty hunter hits Rax with a blaster. After soaking, four damage levels remain. Rax rolls `1d10` — it comes up `2`. The shot hits his cybernetic arm. Because the damage is to a prosthesis, Rax is not at risk of bleeding out, and the penalty only applies to actions using his left arm.
:::

### Cybernetic Sensory Organ (1 pt Merit)

One of your primary sensory organs (eye or ear) has been replaced. Without additional customization, the replacement functions identically to a natural organ, but it has an external mount for connecting recorders or transmitters.

You may take this merit multiple times for multiple organs.

### Cybernetic Uplink (2 pt Merit)

You have a brain implant that allows you to mentally interface with computer networks. Some networks are wireless; others may require a physical plug. If a system was not designed for a neural uplink, you may adapt it with a **Tech (Computers)** roll.

- Difficulty for **Interface** rolls on the network is reduced by **2**
- With a **Programming** roll, you can query and retrieve any information on the system (difficulty based on amount and detail)
- Once connected, you are a **node on the network** — the network has access to your thoughts and memories

### Cybernetic Life Support (1-5 pt Flaw)

One or more of your vital organs has been replaced with a cybernetic implant that keeps you alive. If you ever **botch a Soak roll**, the implant malfunctions:

1. You immediately suffer **Lethal Health Levels equal to the flaw rating**
2. If this reduces you to Wounded or below, you need medical attention to stabilize or you lose additional Health levels hourly
3. You cannot begin recovering Health levels until the damaged life support is repaired or replaced

:::note
Duhla has cybernetic lungs (2 pt flaw). She is stabbed for 3 Lethal damage. Her Stamina soak roll comes up `1, 4, 3` — a botch. Her lungs malfunction, costing 2 more Lethal levels (putting her at Mauled). The damage to her lungs must be repaired before she can recover her own Health levels.
:::

### Clunky Cybernetics (1-5 pt Flaw)

One or more of your cybernetics is of poor quality or does not integrate well with your body. This reduces appropriate Attributes by the flaw rating until the flaw is remediated:

| Cybernetic Type | Reduced Attribute |
| --------------- | ----------------- |
| Limb            | Dexterity         |
| Sensory Organ   | Perception        |
| Uplink          | Wits              |
| Life Support    | Stamina           |

If you have multiple cybernetics, you can apportion this flaw across them. This flaw cannot reduce any Attribute below **1 dot**.

:::note
Rax has a 3 pt Clunky flaw split across his cybernetic eye and arm. He reduces Dexterity by 1 and Perception by 2, leaving him at 1 dot in both until rectified.
:::
