# Backgrounds

Backgrounds represent the narrative assets and resources your character has accumulated before the start of play. They are not skills you roll — they are people, wealth, status, and equipment that shape who you are and what you can call upon.

You have **5 dots** to allocate among the following backgrounds at character creation. Additional dots may be purchased with **Freebie Points** at a cost of **1 point per dot**.

## Spending Freebie Points on Backgrounds

You may raise any background by spending Freebie Points at **1 point per dot**. This is the cheapest way to spend Freebie Points, reflecting that backgrounds are narrative assets rather than personal power.

However, a background dot is only as useful as the Storyteller makes it. A point in **Resources** is always useful; a point in **Allies** depends on whether those allies are in a position to help with the current story.

---

## Splitting Customization

The **Customization** background can be split across multiple items. For example, you might spend 1 dot improving your blaster's range and 2 dots upgrading your ship's shields. Each dot spent on a single item corresponds to the rank description at that level of the scale.

One-for-one attribute trade-offs — such as a weapon trading Range for Damage, or a vehicle trading Durability for Maneuverability — should be allowed for free with the Storyteller's permission.

---

## Droids & Vehicles

Both the **Droid** and **Vehicle** backgrounds require close coordination with the Storyteller. The droid or vehicle you acquire is a story asset — it can be damaged, lost, stolen, or improved during play. Unlike equipment bought with Resources, these are individual characters or vessels with their own histories.
