# Vehicle & Equipment Modifications

The galaxy is full of scroungers, mechanics, and tinkerers upgrading their gear beyond stock specifications. This chapter covers two paths to better equipment: the **Customization background** (character creation) and **in-game upgrades** (Tech rolls).

---

## Customization Background

During character creation, the **Customization** background represents equipment that has been improved beyond its stock attributes. You can split Customization dots across multiple items — one point to a blaster, two points to a vehicle, one point to armor.

### Improvement Table

| Dots      | Improvement Level                                                                    |
| --------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **•**     | Cosmetic/convenience. Paint or body work. Adding a comlink into a cybernetic arm.    |
| **••**    | Easy. +1D or +20% to one attribute. Adding a hidden vibro-knife to a cybernetic arm. |
| **•••**   | Average. +2D or +40%. Adding a hidden hold-out blaster to a cybernetic arm.          |
| **••••**  | Difficult. +3D or +60%. Adding a heavy blaster pistol to a cybernetic arm.           |
| **•••••** | Extraordinary. +4D or +80%. Adding a light repeating blaster to a cybernetic arm.    |

### Free Trade-Offs

With the Storyteller's permission, you can make **one-for-one attribute trade-offs** for free — no Customization dots required:

- A weapon with +20% Range but −1D Damage
- A vehicle with +1D Durability but −1D Maneuverability
- Armor with +1D rating but −1D Dexterity penalty

These are re-configurations rather than true upgrades. The total capability remains roughly the same.

## In-Game Upgrades

During play, a character with the **Tech** knowledge can upgrade vehicles and equipment using **Intelligence + Tech** rolls. Upgrades require:

1. **Time** — determined by the Storyteller (usually downtime between chapters)
2. **Credits** — parts and materials (Storyteller sets cost based on upgrade scope)
3. **Tools** — appropriate tool set reduces difficulty (see below)
4. **A Tech specialty** — can be applied to specific vehicle systems

### Tech Specialties

| Specialty        | Applies To                                              |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Structural**   | Durability, hull modifications                          |
| **Propulsion**   | Speed, Maneuverability, Hyperdrive, repulsorlifts       |
| **Energy Field** | Shields, Sensors, repulsorlift maintenance, comm arrays |
| **Computer**     | Nav computer, sensor data processing, droid programming |
| **Weapons**      | Adding, repairing, or upgrading weapon systems          |

### Upgrade Difficulty Table

Upgrades are made **one step at a time**, and each increase is above the vehicle's **stock or original value**, not the improved value. It is easier and cheaper to upgrade from 1D to 2D than from 4D to 5D.

| Upgrade To                                 | Difficulty |
| ------------------------------------------ | ---------- |
| **1D, 20%, or very minor modification**    | 6          |
| **2D, 40%, or average modification**       | 7          |
| **3D, 60%, or tricky modification**        | 8          |
| **4D, 80%, or difficult modification**     | 9          |
| **5D, 100%, or "impossible" modification** | 10         |

| Equipment Bonus                      | Effect        |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------- |
| Basic Tool Set                       | −1 difficulty |
| Advanced Tool Set (proper specialty) | −2 difficulty |
| Complete Workshop                    | −3 difficulty |

Upgrading systems with a dice pool (Maneuverability, Sensors, Shields, etc.) beyond 5D is at the Storyteller's discretion.

### Quirks

The Storyteller may add **quirks** to upgraded equipment — especially on marginal successes (1 success). A quirk is a downside that makes the upgrade interesting rather than purely numerical:

- Souped-up engines that stall for one turn when pushed too hard
- A weapon upgrade that overheats after three shots
- Shield capacitors that take an extra turn to recharge
- A sensor booster that picks up false echoes in debris fields

### Example: Upgrading a Freighter's Speed

Rax wants to upgrade his Ghtroc freighter "The Goose" during downtime. Its stock Speed is 8, already upgraded once to 10. Increasing it another 20% to 12 is an "average modification" — difficulty 7.

## Droid Upgrades

Droids use the same rules as vehicles for upgrades. Rather than spending experience to improve Attributes (like living characters), a droid's Attributes are upgraded using the Tech roll and upgrade table above.

This reflects the fact that droids are machines — you can't train a droid to be stronger, you install stronger servos.

A droid's **programming** (skills and knowledge) can be upgraded via experience normally, or re-programmed with a Tech (Computers) roll during downtime (see the Repair table in [Durability, Damage & Repair](./durability-damage-repare.mdx) for time requirements).

## Weapon Modifications

The same upgrade rules apply to personal weapons:

| Modification                                        | Difficulty |
| --------------------------------------------------- | ---------- |
| Increase Damage by 1D                               | 7          |
| Increase Range by 20%                               | 6          |
| Add a targeting scope (−1 difficulty at long range) | 7          |
| Reduce weight by 20%                                | 8          |
| Add hidden compartment                              | 6          |

Weapon upgrades are capped at +3D or +60% for hand-held weapons (beyond that, the weapon frame can't handle the stress).

## Tool Sets

| Tool Set              | Cost        | Effect                             | Portability                             |
| --------------------- | ----------- | ---------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **Basic Tool Set**    | 100c        | −1 difficulty to Repair/Tech rolls | Fits in a satchel                       |
| **Advanced Tool Set** | 1,000c each | −2 difficulty to Repair/Tech rolls | Requires a cabinet; needs power         |
| **Complete Workshop** | 10,000c     | −3 difficulty to Repair/Tech rolls | Requires a room; needs own power supply |

Advanced Tool Sets are specialized by category. The six standard types:

- Structural construction and repair
- Propulsion maintenance and repair
- Droid and cybernetic tools
- Computer assembly and repair
- Energy field projector maintenance
- Weapons maintenance and repair

The Storyteller may allow overlap between categories and offer discounts for purchasing multiple sets together.
