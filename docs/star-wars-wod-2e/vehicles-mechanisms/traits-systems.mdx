# Vehicle Traits & Systems

From speeder bikes threading through Coruscant traffic to Star Destroyers exchanging turbolaser broadsides, vehicles in Star Wars follow a different set of rules than characters. This chapter covers how vehicles are built, what their attributes mean, and how to convert WEG stats into this system.

---

## Vehicles vs. Characters

Vehicles are **not characters**. They don't have Attributes (Strength, Dexterity, etc.) or Abilities (Blaster, Pilot, etc.). Instead, they have a set of **vehicle attributes** that describe their performance characteristics.

| Aspect              | Character                                           | Vehicle                                                             |
| ------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Core stats**      | 9 Attributes (Str/Dex/Sta/Cha/Man/App/Per/Int/Wits) | 4+ vehicle attributes (Durability, Maneuverability, Speed, etc.)    |
| **Abilities**       | 30 Talents/Skills/Knowledges                        | None — the pilot's skill matters, not the vehicle's                 |
| **Scale**           | Character (typically)                               | Speeder through Death Star                                          |
| **Creation budget** | Freebie points                                      | None — vehicles are bought with the Vehicle background or Resources |
| **Improvement**     | Experience points                                   | Credits + Tech rolls + Customization background                     |

A vehicle is only as good as its pilot. The vehicle's **Maneuverability** adds dice to the pilot's Pilot roll. Its **Durability** replaces Stamina for soak rolls. All other tasks depend on the crew's Attributes and Abilities.

## Scale

Every vehicle has a **Scale** that determines how it interacts with other scales in combat. This uses the same system described in [Combat Scales](../combat/combat-scales.mdx).

### Vehicle Scale Categories

| Scale           | Examples                                       | How to Tell                                                     |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Speeder**     | Landspeeder, speeder bike, MTT, sail barge     | Flies or hovers, cannot go to space                             |
| **Walker**      | AT-AT, AT-ST, ground vehicles                  | Walking, treaded, wheeled or tracked; cannot fly or hover       |
| **Starfighter** | X-wing, TIE Fighter, A-wing, N-1 starfighter   | Goes to space, &lt;1 ton cargo, no passengers                   |
| **Transport**   | Millennium Falcon, Ghost, lambda shuttle       | Goes to space, carries passengers or cargo in tons, crew &lt;10 |
| **Capital**     | Star Destroyer, Mon Calamari cruiser, Home One | Goes to space, crew of 10+                                      |
| **Death Star**  | Death Star, Starkiller Base                    | Can dock a Capital ship                                         |

### Determining Scale from WEG Stats

Use the vehicle's WEG **Skill** stat as a guide:

- If the skill is **Space Transports**, set Scale to **Transport** regardless of the listed Scale.
- If the skill is **Starfighter Piloting**, set Scale to **Starfighter**.
- If the skill is **Repulsorlift Operation**, set Scale to **Speeder**.

For non-WEG sources, use the "How to Tell" column above.

## Vehicle Attributes

A vehicle's attributes describe its tactical performance. Each is rated on a **1–5 dot scale** (like character attributes), with some exceptions noted below.

### Maneuverability

The vehicle's agility — its ability to turn, dodge, and perform stunts. This adds dice to the pilot's **Pilot** roll in combat or chase situations.

A light, fast starfighter has high Maneuverability. A bulk freighter or walker has low Maneuverability.

**Conversion from WEG:** Use the dice value from the WEG **Maneuverability** stat directly, ignoring pluses. If the value is 6+, cap at 5 and note that the vehicle can **reroll 10s** on Maneuver rolls.

**Opposed to:** Durability. A vehicle can't be both highly maneuverable and heavily armored.

### Durability

The vehicle's structural integrity and ability to absorb damage. This replaces **Stamina** for soak rolls — when a vehicle is hit, the pilot rolls the vehicle's **Durability** at difficulty 6 to soak.

**Conversion from WEG:** Use the dice value from the WEG **Hull** or **Body Strength** stat directly, ignoring pluses. If the value is 6+, cap at 5 and note that the vehicle can **reroll 10s** on Durability rolls.

### Speed

The vehicle's maximum safe operating speed in kmh, converted to an abstract value for quick math:

> **Speed = round(kmh ÷ 100)**

| Vehicle                        | kmh   | Speed |
| ------------------------------ | ----- | ----- |
| AT-AT                          | 60    | 1     |
| X-wing (atmosphere)            | 1,050 | 11    |
| Millennium Falcon (atmosphere) | 1,200 | 12    |
| Speeder bike                   | 400   | 4     |

The Speed value represents the vehicle's safe, no-roll-needed speed. A skilled pilot can push the vehicle faster — each additional increment of Speed beyond the vehicle's rating adds +1 difficulty to the Pilot roll.

**Push example:** <TWWrapper><InlineRoll notation={'7d10\u003e=8'} variant="details" preroll /></TWWrapper> Pushing a speeder bike (Speed 4) to Speed 6 — +2 difficulty for a Pilot roll at difficulty 8.

### Altitude

For vehicles that fly or hover, this describes their maximum operational altitude.

- **Speeders and walkers** — use the WEG **Altitude Range** value directly (e.g., "50m").
- **Starships** — list Altitude as "space". A starship cannot take off or land without functioning repulsorlifts.
- **Ground vehicles** — leave blank (they operate at ground level).

### Comm/Sensors

A merged stat representing the vehicle's communication and sensor capabilities.

**Conversion from WEG:** Use the dice value from the WEG **Scan** sensor mode, ignoring pluses. Cap at 5 if needed.

**Without WEG stats:**

| Vehicle Type           | Sensors |
| ---------------------- | ------- |
| Basic civilian vehicle | 1–2     |
| Scout/exploration ship | 3–4     |
| Military vessel        | 3–5     |

### Sensor Range

The maximum effective range of the vehicle's sensors, in the same abstract units used for Speed and Weapon Ranges.

**Conversion from WEG:** Use the range for the **Scan** mode.

**Without WEG stats:**

| Vehicle Type                  | Sensor Range |
| ----------------------------- | ------------ |
| Speeder / Walker              | 15           |
| Starfighter / light Transport | 25           |
| Transport / Capital ship      | 40+          |

### Hyperdrive

Rates how fast a starship travels in hyperspace. Rated 1–5 dots.

| Dots | WEG Multiplier | Example                                     |
| ---- | -------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| 1    | ×2             | Imperial shuttles, bulk freighters          |
| 2    | ×1.5           | Most transports                             |
| 3    | ×1             | Standard military and civilian              |
| 4    | ×0.75          | Fast couriers, highly modified ships        |
| 5    | ×0.5           | Millennium Falcon, exceptionally fast ships |

A ship with no hyperdrive has 0 dots (cannot enter hyperspace).

### Nav Computer

Describes the ship's navigation capability:

| Type                     | Capability                                                                                               |
| ------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Full nav computer**    | Capital ships, transports — can calculate any hyperspace route                                           |
| **Astromech droid**      | Starfighters with a droid socket — stores 2 pre-programmed jumps per dot of the droid's **Intelligence** |
| **Limited nav computer** | Starfighters without a droid — stores 2 pre-programmed jumps only                                        |
| **None**                 | Ships without hyperdrive                                                                                 |

### Shields

Deflector shields that absorb incoming fire before it hits the hull. Only starships and some military vehicles have shields.

**Conversion from WEG:** Use the dice value from WEG **Shields** stat, ignoring pluses. Cap at 5.

**Without WEG stats:**

| Vehicle Type              | Shields |
| ------------------------- | ------- |
| Starfighter               | 1D      |
| Transport / small Capital | 2D      |
| Large Capital ship        | 3D      |

Shields can be reduced during combat. Each dot of Shields grants one soak box per shield facing (front/rear).

## Weapon Systems

Vehicle weapons are simplified into a few key stats:

| Stat       | Description                                                                                        |
| ---------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Name**   | "Laser Cannons", "Proton Torpedoes", "Turbolaser Batteries"                                        |
| **Arc**    | **Fixed** (fires forward — pilot aims the whole ship) or **Turret** (fires into a 180° hemisphere) |
| **Range**  | Abstract range value — can be doubled with +1 difficulty                                           |
| **Damage** | Dice pool for damage (e.g., 8D) at difficulty 6                                                    |

## Vehicle Sheet

A vehicle uses a simplified sheet with these sections:

1. **Identification** — Name, Model, Owner, Scale
2. **Crew & Capacity** — Crew, Passengers, Cargo, Consumables
3. **Configuration** — Durability, Maneuverability, Speed, Altitude, Comm/Sensors, Hyperdrive, Nav Comp, Shields
4. **Weapons** — Name, Arc, Range, Damage
5. **Damage Track** — 7-level track (Cosmetic → Light → Moderate → Heavy → Severe → Crippled → Wrecked)
6. **Notes** — Customizations, quirks, source reference

## Customizing Vehicles

The **Customization** background (see [Backgrounds](../character/backgrounds.mdx)) allows one-for-one attribute trade-offs for free with Storyteller permission — such as trading 1D of Durability for 1D of Maneuverability.

Spending Customization dots:

| Dots      | Improvement                                        |
| --------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **•**     | Cosmetic/convenience; +1D or +20% to one attribute |
| **••**    | Easy improvement; +2D or +40%                      |
| **•••**   | Average improvement; +3D or +60%                   |
| **••••**  | Difficult improvement; +4D or +80%                 |
| **•••••** | Extraordinary improvement; +5D or +100%            |

These upgrades can also be performed during gameplay with a **Tech roll** (Propulsion specialty for Speed/Maneuverability/Hyperdrive, Energy Field for Shields/Repulsors, Structural for Durability) and sufficient credits.

## Crew Penalties

A vehicle operating without a full crew suffers penalties:

| Crew Level                            | Penalty                         |
| ------------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| **Full crew**                         | No penalty                      |
| **Less than full, at least skeleton** | −1D to all dice pools in combat |
| **Less than skeleton**                | −3D to all dice pools in combat |
