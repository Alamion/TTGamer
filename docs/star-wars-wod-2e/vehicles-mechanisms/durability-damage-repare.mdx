# Durability, Damage & Repair

Vehicles don't heal. They get repaired. When a vehicle takes a hit, its **Durability** replaces Stamina for soak rolls. If the damage gets through, the vehicle's damage track determines how badly it's impaired — and how hard it will be to fix.

---

## Vehicle Damage Track

Like characters, vehicles have a **7-level damage track**. Each level applies a penalty to all the vehicle's dice pools:

| Damage Level | Penalty | Description                                          |
| ------------ | ------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| **Cosmetic** | —       | Scorch marks, scratched paint. No mechanical effect. |
| **Light**    | −1D     | Fittings vibrate loose, minor leaks.                 |
| **Moderate** | −2D     | Circuits short out, welds crack.                     |
| **Heavy**    | −3D     | Structural damage, systems failing.                  |
| **Severe**   | −4D     | Major systems offline, hull integrity compromised.   |
| **Crippled** | −5D     | Barely functional. One more hit from wrecked.        |
| **Wrecked**  | —       | Reactor shutdown. Cannot function.                   |

When a vehicle reaches **Wrecked**, the reactor shuts down. It cannot move, fire weapons, or use shields. Any vehicle that sustains damage beyond Wrecked is **destroyed** — exploded, split in half, or otherwise unrecoverable.

### Damage Type

Unlike characters, vehicles do not take Bashing damage. All damage to a vehicle is effectively **Lethal**. The equivalent of Stun damage comes from **ion weapons** (see below).

---

## Soaking Damage

When a vehicle is hit, the pilot (or the highest-ranking crew member) rolls the vehicle's **Durability** at difficulty 6 to soak.

> **Vehicle Soak:** Roll the vehicle's **Durability** dice at difficulty 6. Each success cancels one damage level.

</TWWrapper>

### Shields

Shields add their rating to the Durability soak roll — but only if the damage exceeds the shield arc's rating.

For each shield arc (Front/Rear):

1. **If damage ≤ shield rating** — no soak needed. The shields absorb the hit entirely.
2. **If damage > shield rating** — add the shield rating to Durability and roll to soak.
3. **After soaking** — if the damage that got through exceeds the shield rating, the shield arc is **overwhelmed**. Reduce that arc's rating by 1.

Shields recharge to full at the start of each new scene, unless the shield generator itself is damaged.

<TWWrapper>
    <InlineRoll notation={'6d10\u003e=6'} variant="details" preroll />
</TWWrapper>

_Shields 2D + Durability 4D = 6D soak roll._

### Inconsequential Vehicles

Named vehicles (those carrying PCs or important NPCs) get soak rolls. Inconsequential vehicles — TIE fighters, speeder bikes, random civilian craft — **do not soak**. They take the full damage from every hit. This keeps combat fast and lets heroes feel powerful against mooks.

---

## General Damage vs. Specific Damage

### General Damage

When a vehicle takes **General Damage**, all systems are compromised equally. The penalty applies to every dice pool the vehicle has — Maneuverability, Sensors, Weapon damage, etc.

General Damage is the default. It's fast and easy, suitable for most combat encounters.

### Specific Damage

Instead of taking a penalty across all systems, a vehicle can opt to take damage to a **single specific system**. This works like amputation for characters — the vehicle avoids the broad penalty but loses a critical function.

The pilot or captain chooses which system is affected. Alternatively, an attacker can **target a specific system** by making a **Wits + Interfaces + Sensors** roll immediately before the attack.

Common systems that can be targeted:

| System                      | Effect When Damaged                                            |
| --------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Durability**              | Hull breach; cannot soak as well                               |
| **Maneuverability**         | Engines, controls, or repulsors damaged; −Maneuverability dice |
| **Comm/Sensors**            | Flying blind; no communications or targeting data              |
| **Hyperdrive/Nav Computer** | Cannot jump to hyperspace                                      |
| **Shields**                 | Shield generator damaged; shields collapse                     |
| **Weapons**                 | Specific weapon system disabled                                |

The Storyteller determines the exact nature of the failure based on damage severity and the dramatic needs of the scene.

### Ion Damage

Ion weapons (such as those used by the Empire on Hoth) deal the vehicle equivalent of Stun damage. An ion hit does not cause lasting structural damage — instead, it temporarily disables systems.

- Ion damage uses the same soak rules but **does not increase the vehicle's damage level**.
- Instead, each success on the ion attack temporarily disables one randomly determined system from the vehicle's Configuration list.
- Disabled systems return after `1d10` turns.
- If an ion hit would have reduced a vehicle to **Wrecked**, the reactor shuts down for `1d10` minutes instead of being destroyed.

---

## Crew Damage

When a vehicle takes damage after soak, the crew may be injured as well. The number of damage levels the vehicle **actually suffered** (after soak) becomes **Bashing damage** to each crew member aboard.

Each crew member rolls Stamina at difficulty 6 to soak the Bashing damage. Anyone who fails marks off health levels normally.

Inconsequential vehicles skip this rule — tracking NPC health on top of vehicle health slows combat down.

---

## Repairing Vehicle Damage

Unlike characters, vehicles cannot heal naturally. The only way to fix a vehicle is through **Repair rolls**. There are two types of repairs: **jury-rigged** (temporary, in-combat fixes) and **permanent** (down-time repairs).

### Repair Table

| Damage Level | Difficulty | Jury-Rig (successes) | Permanent (successes) |
| ------------ | ---------- | -------------------- | --------------------- |
| **Cosmetic** | 7          | —                    | 2 × scale             |
| **Light**    | 8          | 3                    | 5 × scale             |
| **Moderate** | 8          | 6                    | 10 × scale            |
| **Heavy**    | 9          | 9                    | 15 × scale            |
| **Severe**   | 9          | 12                   | 25 × scale            |
| **Crippled** | 10         | 15                   | 50 × scale            |
| **Wrecked**  | 10         | 18                   | 100 × scale           |

### Scale Multipliers

Multiply the required successes by the vehicle's scale:

| Scale                | Multiplier |
| -------------------- | ---------- |
| Speeder              | ×1         |
| Walker / Starfighter | ×2         |
| Transport            | ×3         |
| Capital              | ×5         |
| Death Star           | ×10        |

### Equipment Bonuses

Having the right tools makes repairs easier:

| Equipment                            | Bonus         |
| ------------------------------------ | ------------- |
| Basic Tool Set                       | −1 difficulty |
| Advanced Tool Set (proper specialty) | −2 difficulty |
| Complete Workshop                    | −3 difficulty |

A Repair specialty (Structural, Propulsion, Energy Field, Computer, Weapons) can be applied to specific vehicle systems.

### Jury-Rigged Repairs

Jury-rigging gets a specific system working again **during combat**. It does not improve the vehicle's overall damage level.

- Roll **Wits + Repair** each turn against the difficulty on the table.
- Accumulate the required successes. Once reached, the damaged system functions normally.
- The vehicle's damage level **does not improve**. The next hit still increases damage as normal.
- Cosmetic damage cannot be jury-rigged (there's nothing to bodge).

<TWWrapper>
    <InlineRoll notation={'7d10\u003e=8'} variant="details" preroll />
</TWWrapper>

_Wits 4 + Repair 3 = 7D at difficulty 8 to jury-rig Moderate damage._

### Permanent Repairs

Permanent repairs happen during downtime. Repairing a vehicle occurs **one damage level at a time** — fix Heavy first, then Moderate, then Light, and so on.

- Roll **Intelligence + Repair** each hour against the difficulty on the table.
- Multiply required successes by the vehicle's Scale Multiplier.
- Multiple technicians can work simultaneously, each making their own roll each hour.
- Failed rolls mean no progress that hour — try again next hour.
- A **botch** means the technician is stuck. They cannot make further repairs until the situation changes (better tools, skill increase, or another character improves the vehicle's condition).

#### Coordinating NPC Technicians

A PC can coordinate NPC technicians with a **Wits + Command** roll at difficulty 6. Multiply the successes by the vehicle's Scale Multiplier to determine how many NPCs can be utilized. Each NPC contributes 1 automatic success per hour, provided the PC in charge makes their Repair roll.

---

## Droid Repair Parallel

Droids recover from damage the same way vehicles are repaired. A damaged droid requires:

- **Jury-rigged repairs** — a **Wits + Repair** roll during combat, with the same difficulty and success thresholds.
- **Permanent repairs** — an **Intelligence + Repair** roll during downtime.

Droids do not heal naturally. A droid at Incapacitated must be repaired before it can function again. However, droids do not need to be stabilized to prevent additional health loss (unlike living characters).
