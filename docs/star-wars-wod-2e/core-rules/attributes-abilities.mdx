## Attributes

The nine Attributes define your character's raw capabilities, divided evenly across three categories of three Traits each. They are rolled as part of Dice Pools — almost every action pairs an Attribute with an Ability.

**Starting distribution:** Allocate **7 / 5 / 3** dots across Physical, Social, and Mental (your choice of Primary, Secondary, Tertiary). Every Attribute starts with 1 inherent dot, so nothing can ever drop to 0. The natural maximum for a starting character is 5.

### Physical

Physical Traits describe how strong, dexterous, and sturdy a character is. They are the primary Traits of an action-oriented character, concerning only the strengths and weaknesses of the body.

### Social

Social Traits describe appearance, charm, and familiarity with the minds of others. They are vital in determining first impressions, leadership ability, and the nature of your interactions with others.

### Mental

Mental Traits represent your character's total mental capacity, including memory, perception, and the ability to learn and think.

## Abilities

The thirty Abilities (Talents, Skills, Knowledges) are the trained and instinctive competencies your character has developed. They complement Attributes in Dice Pools — where an Attribute provides raw capacity, an Ability provides technique and training.

**Starting distribution:** **13 / 9 / 5** dots across Talents, Skills, and Knowledges. Unlike Attributes, Abilities start at 0 — you must invest dots to gain any proficiency. The natural maximum at character creation is 3.

### Talents

Talents describe untrained, intuitive Abilities. They can never be learned through study — only through direct experience, usually during a story. Because Talents are so natural and instinctive, every character is assumed to have some small capacity in each one. If you use a Talent you do not possess, you roll the base Attribute with no penalty.

### Skills

Skills are Abilities learned through apprenticeships or rigorous training. If you try to use a Skill but have no rating in it, the difficulty of the roll increases by **+1** — you are untrained in its techniques.

### Knowledges

Knowledges require the rigorous application of the mind, not the body. Only Mental Attributes can be used to modify Knowledge rolls. If you do not have a Knowledge dot in a given field, you generally cannot even attempt a roll involving it — though the Storyteller may rule otherwise for trivial information anyone might know.

## Secondary Abilities

Beyond the thirty core Abilities, players may define Secondary Abilities — more specialized traits that allow finer character definition. These are often subcategories of broader Abilities (e.g., Sense Deception narrowing Empathy). The character sheet includes blank spaces for this purpose.

Storytellers may handle Secondary Abilities in one of two ways:

- **Lower difficulty** — Using a more specialized Ability lowers the difficulty of relevant rolls, making it more powerful in its niche.
- **Lower cost** — Each dot spent on a Secondary Ability during character creation grants two dots instead of one, and raising them costs only 1 Freebie Point per dot (or the current rating in XP after play begins).

This list is not definitive — Storytellers are free to create other Abilities as they see fit.
