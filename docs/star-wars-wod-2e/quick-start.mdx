# Quick Start: Play in 15 Minutes

:::tip[Before you begin]
Open the character sheet manager in another tab at **[/universal_sheet](/universal_sheet)** — you can keep it open while reading this guide to reference fields in real-time.
:::

## What You Need

- **This guide** — your structural map for the initial session.
- **The character sheet** — open and ready in a separate tab at `/universal_sheet`.
- **Ten-sided dice (d10)** — physical or digital dice pools. Unlike traditional Star Wars WEG systems, this hybrid system relies entirely on World of Darkness d10 mechanics.
- **A Group** — 2 to 5 players to portray heroes, and 1 Game Master (GM) to narrate the galaxy.

---

## The Core Mechanics

Every time your character attempts a tactical action or faces an uncertain obstacle, the loop boils down to one simple concept:

1. **Gather your dice pool** — Look at your character sheet and add your relevant **Attribute** dots to your relevant **Ability** (Skill/Talent/Knowledge) dots.
2. **Roll ten-sided dice (d10s)** — Roll a total number of d10s equal to that combined pool. The GM sets a standard **Difficulty** (universally 6 for most galactic tasks).
3. **Subtract Ones (The Rule of One)** — Every die showing a `1` cancels out exactly one success. Remove both the "success" die and the "1" die from your evaluation.
4. **Evaluate successes** — Every remaining die that shows a 6 or higher counts as a **success**. Meeting or exceeding the required successes (typically 1 success for standard tasks) means you accomplish your goal. Extra successes indicate exceptional flair or turn into more lethal impact!

> **The Botch Guardrail:** If your roll yields **zero successes** and at least one `1`, a catastrophic disaster occurs (a **Botch**). If your successes are canceled out to exactly 0 by cards of `1`, it is a regular failure, not a botch.

**Example:** _Jax Vorn_ tries to bypass a security panel. His **Dexterity** is 3 and his **Security** skill is 2, giving him an active dice pool of `5d10`. The GM sets the standard difficulty at 6. He rolls:

```text
🎲 3, 🎲 5, 🎲 6, 🎲 9, 🎲 1

```

_Evaluation:_ The `6` and `9` are successes (2 total), but the `1` cancels one out. Jax is left with **1 success** — a marginal victory! The lock cycles open just as the patrol passes.

:::info[Extended Actions]
If a task is mechanically massive or time-consuming (like repairing a hyperdrive or slicing an Imperial mainframe), the GM will use an **Extended Action**. Instead of a harder difficulty, you must accumulate a **total number of successes** (e.g., 10 or 20 successes) over multiple consecutive turns.
:::

:::tip[Untrained Actions]
If you don't possess the required Ability, you can often still roll your raw base **Attribute** pool. However, if the task requires highly specialized training under **Knowledges**, the GM may forbid the roll entirely without the matching skill.
:::

:::note[Difficulty Reference Guide]

| Difficulty | Standard Situation                                                                    |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **3**      | Easy — reading a data-pad in perfect lighting                                         |
| **4**      | Routine — piloting a landspeeder down an empty spaceport lane                         |
| **5**      | Straightforward — slicing a low-grade commercial security electronic lock             |
| **6**      | Standard — most regular galactic tasks under tactical pressure                        |

## Step 1: Create Your Character

### 1. Initialize the Template

Just click the <NewCharacterButton /> button right here. Or, you can navigate to the digital sheet manager at `/universal_sheet` and do the same thing.

### 2. Fill in the Basics

Navigate to the `Base` block and fill out your identifying concept traits:

- `Name`: Who are you in the galaxy? (e.g., _Jax Vorn_)
- `Concept`: Your elevator pitch background (e.g., _Corellian Smuggler_, _Fugitive Padawan_)
- `Species`: Pick your species template (e.g., _Human_, _Wookiee_, _Twi'lek_)

<DocsComponentWrapper><BaseBlock accentColor="primary"/></DocsComponentWrapper>

### 3. Distribute Your Attribute Dots

Scroll to the `Attributes` block. Your core attributes are split into three structural columns: **Physical**, **Social**, and **Mental**.
Rank these columns based on your character concept. Distribute your starting dot pools using the **7 / 5 / 3** priority budget:

- **Primary Column:** Allocate **7 dots** across its three attributes.
- **Secondary Column:** Allocate **5 dots** across its three attributes.
- **Tertiary Column:** Allocate **3 dots** across its three attributes.
- _Note: Every attribute automatically starts with 1 inherent dot. The maximum rating for a starting character is 5._

<DocsComponentWrapper><AttributeBlock accentColor="secondary"/></DocsComponentWrapper>

### 4. Distribute Your Ability Dots

Scroll to the `Skills` block, which houses your fields of specialized training across three structural columns: **Talents** (intuitive traits), **Skills** (trained actions), and **Knowledges** (academic study).
Prioritize these columns and distribute your dots using the **13 / 9 / 5** budget:

- **Primary Column:** Allocate **13 dots**
- **Secondary Column:** Allocate **9 dots**.
- **Tertiary Column:** Allocate **5 dots**.
- _Note: Every ability starts with 0 inherent dot. The maximum rating for a starting character is 3._

<DocsComponentWrapper><SkillBlock accentColor="primary"/></DocsComponentWrapper>

### 5. Establish Advantages & Virtues

- **Backgrounds:** In the `Advantages` block, click `Add Background` to allocate your **5 starting dots** into narrative vectors like `Contacts`, `Resources`, or `Vehicle`.
- **Virtues:** In the `Force` block, locate your core ethics: `Conscience`, `Passion`, and `Self Control`. Distribute **7 dots** among them (each starts with 1 dot inherently).

<br/>
> **Derived Calculations:** The sheet will automatically calculate your secondary pools based on your starting virtues. Your maximum `Willpower` pool equals `Passion + Self Control`. Your starting `Dark Side Resistance` equals `5 + Conscience − Passion`.

### 6. Fine-Tune with Freebie Points

You have **15 Freebie Points** to buy additional advantages or custom traits. Spend them using the structural reference below:

- **Attribute:** 5 points per dot
- **Ability (Skill/Talent/Knowledge):** 2 points per dot
- **Virtue:** 2 points per dot
- **Force Skills / Powers:** 3 points per dot/power _(Requires the `Force Sensitive` Merit)_
- **Backgrounds / Merits:** 1 point per rating dot

## Step 2: Understand Your Sheet

Your digital layout at `/universal_sheet` is segmented into clear, interactive functional zones designed to reflect live gameplay data:

- **Base Info:** Tracks structural traits like your `Name`, `Species`, `Nature`, and `Demeanor`.
- **Attributes:** Contains your raw innate capabilities split across **Physical** (e.g., **Dexterity**), **Social** (e.g., **Charisma**), and **Mental** (e.g., **Perception**).
- **Abilities/Skills:** Houses your active modifiers split into **Talents**, **Skills**, and **Knowledges** (such as **Blaster**, **Pilot**, or **Astrogation**).
- **Advantages & Force:** Manages narrative backgrounds, secondary pools like your active temporary `Force Points`, and active `Force Skills` (`Control`, `Sense`, `Telekinesis`).
- **Body & Health Track:** Your active inventory tracking, weapon damage profiles, and your **7-level condition monitor** ranging from healthy down to `Incapacitated`.

:::tip[Quick Reference: Common Dice Pools]

| Action | Dice Pool |
| --- | --- |
| Shoot a blaster | **Dexterity** + **Blaster** |
| Dodge incoming fire | **Dexterity** + **Dodge** |
| Sneak past guards | **Dexterity** + **Stealth** |
| Persuade an NPC | **Charisma** + **Diplomacy** |
| Notice a hidden threat | **Perception** + **Alertness** |
| Repair a broken system | **Dexterity** + **Repair** |
| Jump to hyperspace | **Intelligence** + **Astrogation** |
| Resist Force influence | **Self Control** (standalone roll) |

:::

## Step 3: Combat Basics

When talks break down, blasters are unholstered, and lightsabers ignite, action moves into high-stakes combat rounds.

### 1. Establish Turn Order (Initiative)

Before anyone pulls a trigger, establish the active turn order for the scene by rolling your **Wits** + **Alertness** pool. Characters act in descending order of successes. Ties resolve simultaneously.

### 2. Take Your Turn Actions

On your initiative count, your character can move a reasonable distance (take cover or reposition) and perform **one primary action** (such as firing a blaster weapon, treating an ally's wounds with medicine, or channeling a Force power).

### 3. Resolve the Attack Roll

When launching a ranged strike, roll your **Dexterity** + **Blaster** pool against a standard target baseline difficulty of 6. If the target actively attempts to clear the line of fire, they can declare an immediate reaction roll using **Dexterity** + **Dodge**. Every success they roll subtracts exactly one success from your attack roll. If your remaining successes meet or exceed the difficulty, you score a clean hit.

### 4. Calculate Damage & Soak

If your attack connects, roll your weapon's specific damage pool (e.g., a standard blaster rifle rolls 6 damage dice) at difficulty 6. Every success inflicts one wound level on the target.

- **The Soak Check:** To survive hazardous energy fire, heroic player characters (PCs) and critical named NPCs can immediately make a Soak roll using their **Stamina** dots at difficulty 6 to absorb incoming trauma.
- **The Heroic Rule Barrier:** To preserve a cinematic scale, heroes can always attempt to soak Lethal blaster fire with raw **Stamina**. However, plasma ordnance is destructive: if a Lethal attack successfully hits your character, you **must always sustain a minimum of 1 wound level**, regardless of how many soak successes you roll. Unnamed fodder NPCs cannot soak Lethal damage at all and take full impact.

### 5. Track Health Damage Types

Mark off sustained trauma on your 7-level health monitor from top to bottom based on the damage nature:

- **Bashing damage (`/`):** Blunt trauma, fists, and minor falls. Soaked by **Stamina**. Recovers at a natural rate of 1 level per hour.
- **Lethal damage (`X`):** Blaster bolts, explosive ordinance, and lightsabers. Soaked only by heroes and named targets (minimum 1 sustained). Recovers slowly over weeks or months unless treated inside a tech-fueled Bacta Tank.
- **Stun damage (`/`):** Non-lethal electrical bursts or ion weapons (vs droids). Soaked like Bashing and recovers fast at a rate of **1 level per active turn**.

:::caution[Catastrophic Stun & Soak Fails]

- **Stun Overwhelm:** If Stun damage boxes fill your track down to `Incapacitated`, your character immediately drops unconscious (or reboots, if a Droid) for `1d10` minutes. If you take Bashing or Lethal trauma while stunned, quick recovery is halted.
- **Botching on Soak:** If a player rolls a **Botch** on any Soak check, their character immediately collapses unconscious or enters an involuntary emergency system reboot for `1d10` minutes from acute systemic shock, regardless of the damage value inflicted.
  :::

### 6. Apply Wound Penalties

Accumulating severe trauma across your health levels inflicts escalating dice penalties (e.g., Hurt = `-1`, Crippled = `-5`) that directly subtract dice from all future action pools as your hero fights through the pain.
