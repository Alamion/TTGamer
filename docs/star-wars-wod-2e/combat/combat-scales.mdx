# Combat Scales

The galaxy is full of things that are much bigger — or much smaller — than a person. A Rancor swatting at an Ewok and an X-wing strafing a Star Destroyer use the same mechanic: **combat scales**.

---

## The Scale Chart

Find the attacker's scale and the target's scale on this chart. Count the rows between them — each is a **step**.

<TWWrapper>
    <ScaleChart />
</TWWrapper>

- **Same scale** — no modifier. Normal combat rules.
- **Attacker is larger** (higher on the chart) — harder to hit (+1 diff per step), easier to damage (−1 diff per step).
- **Attacker is smaller** (lower on the chart) — easier to hit (−1 diff per step), harder to damage (+1 diff per step).

---

## Applying the Modifier

Count the steps between the two scales. For each step:

| Direction              | To Hit                            | To Damage                            |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Larger attacks smaller | **+1 difficulty** (harder to hit) | **−1 difficulty** (easier to damage) |
| Smaller attacks larger | **−1 difficulty** (easier to hit) | **+1 difficulty** (harder to damage) |

### Larger vs. Smaller — Attacking Down

A larger attacker has trouble hitting a small, nimble target, but if they connect, the damage is devastating.

<TWWrapper>
    <InlineRoll notation={'6d10\u003e=8'} variant="details" preroll />
</TWWrapper>

A Rancor (Walker) attacking an Ewok (Character) — 2 steps down. Difficulty to hit is **6 + 2 = 8**. If it hits, difficulty to damage is **6 − 2 = 4**.

### Smaller vs. Larger — Attacking Up

A smaller attacker finds it easier to land hits on a huge target, but their weapons struggle to do meaningful damage.

<TWWrapper>
    <InlineRoll notation={'8d10\u003e=4'} variant="details" preroll />
</TWWrapper>

An Ewok shooting a bowcaster at the Rancor (Character vs. Walker, 2 steps up). Difficulty to hit is **6 − 2 = 4**. Difficulty to damage is **6 + 2 = 8**.

## Examples

### Rancor vs. Ewok (Melee)

| Factor    | Value                                                           |
| --------- | --------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Scales    | Walker (Rancor) → Character (Ewok)                              |
| Steps     | **2**                                                           |
| To hit    | 6 + 2 = **8** (Rancor finds it hard to swat the nimble Ewok)    |
| To damage | 6 − 2 = **4** (if it connects, the massive claws crush through) |

### X-wing vs. Star Destroyer (Space Combat)

| Factor    | Value                                                 |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Scales    | Starfighter (X-wing) → Capital (Star Destroyer)       |
| Steps     | **2**                                                 |
| To hit    | 6 − 2 = **4** (the Star Destroyer is a huge target)   |
| To damage | 6 + 2 = **8** (laser cannons barely scratch the hull) |

The X-wing would need proton torpedoes (high damage) or a targeted run on a vulnerable point (shield generator, bridge) to have a real effect.

### A-Wing vs. TIE Fighter (Same Scale)

| Factor    | Value                     |
| --------- | ------------------------- |
| Scales    | Starfighter → Starfighter |
| Steps     | **0**                     |
| To hit    | standard **6**            |
| To damage | standard **6**            |

A fair fight. Pilot skill determines the outcome.

## Determining Scale

Not sure what scale something is? Use these guidelines:

| Scale           | Examples                       | How to Tell                                        |
| --------------- | ------------------------------ | -------------------------------------------------- |
| **Death Star**  | Death Star, Starkiller Base    | Can dock a Capital ship                            |
| **Capitol**     | Super Star Destroyer, Home One | Crew of 10+, can land starfighters                 |
| **Transport**   | Millennium Falcon, Ghost       | Crew < 10, carries passengers/cargo, goes to space |
| **Starfighter** | X-wing, TIE Fighter, A-wing    | Goes to space, no passengers, < 1 ton cargo        |
| **Walker**      | AT-AT, AT-ST, Rancor           | Ground vehicle on legs/treads, cannot fly          |
| **Speeder**     | Landspeeder, speeder bike, MTT | Flies or hovers, cannot go to space                |
| **Character**   | Human, Wookie, Ewok, most PCs  | A person-sized being                               |
| **Vermin**      | Kowakian monkey-lizard, mynock | Smaller than a person, pest-sized                  |

Creatures larger than a person but smaller than a walker (e.g., a Rancor or Rathtar) are **Walker** scale. Creatures smaller than a person are **Vermin** scale.

---

## Vehicles & Combat Scales

Vehicle combat uses the same scale system. A starfighter attacking a capital ship follows the "attacking up" rules — easier to hit, harder to damage.

Vehicle-scale combat adds **Maneuverability** (dice bonus to pilot rolls) and **Durability** (used for soak instead of Stamina). A vehicle's soak roll uses its Durability rating at difficulty 6, modified by scale.
