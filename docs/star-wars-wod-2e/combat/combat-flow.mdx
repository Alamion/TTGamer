# Combat Flow

When blasters start firing and lightsabers ignite, the game shifts into structured rounds. Here is how combat unfolds.

---

## 1. Initiative

At the start of combat, each character rolls **Wits + Alertness** at difficulty **6**. Characters act in descending order of successes. Ties are resolved simultaneously — both declare actions and resolve together.

<TWWrapper>
    <InlineRoll notation={'4d10\u003e=6f=1'} variant="details" preroll /> — a Wits 3 + Alertness 1
    initiative roll
</TWWrapper>

Initiative is rolled **once** at the top of combat, not every turn. The order remains fixed for the duration of the scene unless a character deliberately holds or delays their action.

:::note[Surprise & Ambush]
If one side catches the other unaware, the ambushing characters get a **free turn** before initiative is rolled. After the surprise round, roll initiative normally.
:::

---

## 2. Turn Structure

Each turn, a character may take **one movement** and **one action**.

### Movement

Move a reasonable distance — duck behind cover, close to melee range, reposition for a better angle. In tactical terms, this is roughly **3‑5 meters per turn** for most characters. Faster species or open terrain may allow more.

### Actions

The primary action covers anything that requires a roll:

| Action Type              | Examples                                              |
| ------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| **Attack**               | Fire a blaster, swing a vibro-blade, throw a punch    |
| **Active Defense**       | Declare a Dodge reaction                              |
| **Use Skill**            | Repair a panel, slice a terminal, administer medicine |
| **Activate Force Power** | Concentrate on a Force ability                        |
| **Miscellaneous**        | Draw a weapon, throw a switch, shout an order         |

### Splitting Your Pool

To do more than one thing in a turn, split your smallest relevant pool evenly. Each action gets its own roll. Movement costs **1 die per 3 yards** from whichever pool you choose.

</TWWrapper>

See [Dice Pools — Splitting Pools](../core-rules/dice-pools.mdx#splitting-pools-for-multiple-actions) for the full rules.

## 3. Ranged Attack Resolution

### Step 1: Declare and Roll

Roll **Dexterity + Blaster** at difficulty **6**. Each success is a potential hit.

<TWWrapper>
    <CharRoll attr="Dexterity" skill="Blaster" variant="details" />
</TWWrapper>

### Step 2: Active Defense (Dodge)

If the target has not yet acted this turn, they may declare a **Dodge reaction** — rolling **Dexterity + Dodge** at difficulty **6**. Each success on the Dodge roll subtracts **one success** from the attacker's roll, just like the Rule of One.

<TWWrapper>
    <CharRoll attr="Dexterity" skill="Dodge" variant="details" />
</TWWrapper>

If the target has already acted this turn, they cannot declare a Dodge but may still use **passive defense** — the GM may increase the attack difficulty based on cover, distance, or movement (typically +1 to +3).

Once a character declares a Dodge, they cannot take any other action that turn — all their focus is on avoiding the attack.

### Step 3: Determine Hit

If the attacker has **at least 1 success** remaining after the Dodge subtraction, the attack hits. Extra successes beyond the first do not increase damage directly (unless using called shots or special ammunition) — they simply mean a cleaner hit.

### Step 4: Roll Damage

Roll the weapon's damage dice at difficulty **6**. Each success inflicts **one health level** of damage.

<TWWrapper>
    <InlineRoll notation={'5d10\u003e=6'} variant="details" preroll /> — a standard blaster pistol
    (5D damage)
</TWWrapper>

### Step 5: Soak

The target rolls **Stamina** at difficulty **6**. Each success cancels one health level of damage.

<TWWrapper>
    <CharRoll attr="Stamina" variant="details" />
</TWWrapper>

:::caution[The Heroic Rule Barrier]
PCs and named NPCs can soak Lethal damage, but they **always suffer a minimum of 1 wound level** regardless of soak successes. Unnamed NPCs (fodder) **cannot soak Lethal damage at all** — they take full damage from every hit.
:::

### Step 6: Apply Damage

Mark damage on the health track. Apply wound penalties to future rolls.

| Damage Type | Mark | Can Soak?               | Min Wound |
| ----------- | ---- | ----------------------- | --------- |
| **Bashing** | `/`  | Everyone                | None      |
| **Lethal**  | `X`  | PCs + named NPCs only   | **1**     |
| **Stun**    | `•`  | Everyone (like Bashing) | None      |

## 4. Melee Attack Resolution

Melee uses the same structure as ranged, with two differences:

1. **Pool:** Roll **Dexterity + Melee** (or **Strength + Brawl** for unarmed).
2. **No Dodge penalty:** A character who declares a Dodge against melee still loses their action — but they may also attempt to parry (see [Dueling](#6-dueling-rules-optional)) instead of dodging.

If the target is within arm's reach, there is no range consideration. Closing to melee requires movement.

<TWWrapper>
    <CharRoll attr="Dexterity" skill="Melee" variant="details" />
</TWWrapper>

### Unarmed Combat

Brawling uses **Strength + Brawl** and deals **Bashing** damage equal to **Strength + 1**. Characters with the **Natural Weapons** merit deal Lethal damage instead.

## 5. Reactions

Characters have one **reaction** per turn — a reflexive response to an immediate threat.

| Reaction      | Trigger                          | Effect                                                         |
| ------------- | -------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Dodge**     | Being attacked (ranged or melee) | Roll Dexterity + Dodge; successes subtract from attacker's hit |
| **Parry**     | Being attacked (melee only)      | Roll Dexterity + Melee; successes subtract from attacker's hit |
| **Intercept** | An opponent moving past you      | Roll to stop them; counts as your action next turn             |

Declaring a reaction uses your action for the turn. You cannot Dodge or Parry if you have already acted.

## 6. Dueling Rules (Optional)

For climactic one-on-one melee battles — lightsaber duels, vibro-blade showdowns, dramatic fistfights — the standard "roll until someone hits" approach can feel flat. For those scenes, use the **Extended + Resisted** framework below.

For most melee attacks, the standard combat rules work fine.

### Setup

1. Roll **Initiative** (Wits + Alertness). The winner becomes the **Attacker**; the loser becomes the **Defender**.
2. These roles persist until the Defender seizes the initiative.
3. Both duelists begin an **Extended Action**, accumulating successes turn by turn toward their goal.

### Goal

| Role         | Needs                                           | Result on reaching goal                                                   |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Attacker** | Successes equal to the **Defender's Willpower** | Roll a standard Melee attack — damage, soak, and penalties apply normally |
| **Defender** | Successes equal to the **Attacker's Willpower** | Seize initiative — roles reverse, successes reset                         |

### Pacing & Duration

A typical duel lasts **3–5 turns per phase** — comparable to a few rounds of standard combat.

- If both duelists are evenly matched (similar Willpower, similar Melee), expect ~3 turns to reach the goal at ~2 successes per turn.
- Smart duelists exploit the opponent's **weakest Attribute group**. If you duel using an Attribute from the opponent's **tertiary** group (the one they spent the fewest dots on), reduce the target by 3. If you use their **primary** group, increase it by 3. This rewards creative Attribute+Ability pairings:
    - **Perception + Melee** to study patterns (Mental vs. a brute)
    - **Strength + Melee** to overpower (Physical vs. a scholar)
    - **Wits + Melee** to outthink (Mental vs. a brawler)

### Rolling

Each turn, both combatants roll an appropriate Attribute + Melee. The Storyteller encourages creative pairings — **Perception + Melee** to study an opponent's patterns, **Strength + Melee** to overpower, **Wits + Melee** to outthink.

<TWWrapper>
    <CharRoll attr="Dexterity" skill="Melee" variant="details" />
</TWWrapper>

### Resolving the Duel

When the **Attacker** reaches their goal, they immediately roll a standard Melee attack — on a hit, proceed to damage and soak as normal. The Attacker can continue making standard attacks each subsequent turn as long as they maintain initiative.

When the **Defender** reaches their goal, they seize the initiative. Roles reverse. Accumulated successes reset to zero. Combat continues with the new Attacker pressing their advantage.

### Breaking the Rhythm

Either duelist may, on their turn, forego the Melee roll and take a different action — kick a piece of furniture, throw sand, activate a piece of equipment. This forfeits progress on the extended action but can create tactical openings.

## 7. Cover & Positioning

| Situation                          | Effect                                      |
| ---------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| **Light cover** (crate, table)     | +1 difficulty to hit                        |
| **Heavy cover** (wall, blast door) | +2 difficulty to hit                        |
| **Prone / ducking**                | +1 difficulty to hit; -1 die on own attacks |
| **Target running**                 | +1 difficulty to hit                        |
| **Point blank** (within 2m)        | -1 difficulty to hit                        |

Cover is the difference between life and death in a blaster fight. Smart positioning keeps you alive.

For a full breakdown of success probabilities, botch rates, and how to calibrate difficulty for your table, see the **[Difficulty & Success Reference](/docs/star-wars-wod-2e/gm/difficulty-reference)**.

## 8. Wound Penalties

As damage accumulates, every action suffers:

| Health Level  | Penalty    |
| ------------- | ---------- |
| Bruised       | —          |
| Hurt          | **-1**     |
| Injured       | **-2**     |
| Wounded       | **-3**     |
| Mauled        | **-4**     |
| Crippled      | **-5**     |
| Incapacitated | Cannot act |

These dice are subtracted directly from every dice pool. A character at Wounded (-3) with Dexterity 5 + Blaster 3 = 8 dice rolls only **5 dice**.

## Summary: Combat Round Flowchart

```
1. Roll Initiative (Wits + Alertness)
         ↓
2. Highest success acts first
         ↓
3. On your turn:
   ├─ Move (3-5m, or split pool for more)
   └─ Action:
       ├─ Attack → Roll pool → Target may Dodge → Damage → Soak
       ├─ Use skill → Roll as normal
       ├─ Activate power → Follow power rules
       └─ Hold action → Act later in turn order
         ↓
4. Repeat until combat ends
```
