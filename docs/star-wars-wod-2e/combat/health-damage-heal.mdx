# Health, Damage & Healing

Every character has a 7-level health track. As damage accumulates, wound penalties apply to all dice pools — and at the far end, Incapacitation takes you out of the fight.

---

## The Health Track

The health track has seven levels, each with an associated wound penalty:

| Level             | Penalty | Description                                   |
| ----------------- | ------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| **Bruised**       | —       | Minor bumps and scuffs. No mechanical effect. |
| **Hurt**          | −1      | A solid hit. You're feeling it.               |
| **Injured**       | −2      | Bleeding, bruised, but still fighting.        |
| **Wounded**       | −3      | Serious trauma. Every action costs you.       |
| **Mauled**        | −4      | Barely standing. Adrenaline and instinct.     |
| **Crippled**      | −5      | One more hit and you're down.                 |
| **Incapacitated** | —       | Cannot act. Unconscious or system shutdown.   |

## Damage Types

There are three damage types, each marked differently on the health track.

### Bashing Damage (`/`)

Blunt trauma, fists, falls, collisions. Soaked normally.

<TWWrapper>
    <HealthViewer levels={['slash', 'slash', 'slash', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty']} />
</TWWrapper>

Three levels of Bashing damage — Bruised, Hurt, and Injured marked with slashes.

- **Can soak:** Everyone
- **Minimum wound:** None
- **Recovery:** 1 level per hour

### Lethal Damage (`X`)

Blaster bolts, lightsabers, explosives, vibro-blades. The real danger.

<TWWrapper>
    <HealthViewer levels={['cross', 'cross', 'cross', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty']} />
</TWWrapper>

Three levels of Lethal damage — Bruised, Hurt, and Injured marked with crosses.

- **Can soak:** PCs and named NPCs only (Heroic Rule)
- **Minimum wound:** **1** — even a perfect soak leaves at least one wound
- **Recovery:** Days to months

### Stun Damage (`•`)

Stun settings on blasters, ion weapons (vs. droids), electrical bursts.

<TWWrapper>
    <HealthViewer levels={['slash', 'slash', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty']} />
</TWWrapper>

Two levels of Stun damage — marked with slashes, same visual as Bashing.

- **Can soak:** Everyone (like Bashing)
- **Minimum wound:** None
- **Recovery:** 1 level per turn
- **If Incapacitated:** Unconscious for **1d10 minutes**

Stun damage replaces V:TM's Aggravated damage. It is marked and tracked like Bashing, but recovers much faster.

### Mixed Damage

Multiple damage types can stack on the same track. The penalty is always from the first non-empty level.

<TWWrapper>
    <HealthViewer levels={['cross', 'slash', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty']} />
</TWWrapper>

Lethal at Bruised, Bashing at Hurt — penalty is 0 (Bruised is the first marked level).

## Taking Damage

### Step 1: The Attack Lands

After an attack hits and the damage is rolled (see [Combat Flow](./combat-flow.mdx)), the number of successes on the damage roll equals the number of health levels lost.

### Step 2: Soak

Roll **Stamina** at difficulty **6**. Each success cancels one health level of damage.

<TWWrapper>
    <InlineRoll notation={'3d10\u003e=6'} variant="details" preroll />
</TWWrapper>

| Damage Type | Who Can Soak     | Minimum |
| ----------- | ---------------- | ------- |
| **Bashing** | Everyone         | 0       |
| **Lethal**  | PCs + named NPCs | **1**   |
| **Stun**    | Everyone         | 0       |

:::caution[Heroic Rule Barrier]
PCs and named NPCs can soak Lethal damage, but **always suffer a minimum of 1 wound level** — no matter how many soak successes they roll. This preserves the danger of blaster fire while keeping heroes in the fight.

Unnamed NPCs (fodder stormtroopers, random thugs) **cannot soak Lethal damage at all**.
:::

### Step 3: Apply Wound Penalties

The penalty from the first non-empty health level applies to all dice pools. A character with Bashing at Bruised (penalty 0) fights at full capacity. A character with Lethal at Wounded (penalty -3) loses 3 dice from every roll.

### Step 4: Check for Incapacitation

If damage fills the track to Incapacitated, the character collapses. Additional Lethal damage to an Incapacitated character can be fatal — see [Stabilization](#stabilization).

### Botching on Soak

Overrides the standard WoD exception: Soak rolls **can** botch. If you botch a Soak roll, you fall unconscious (or reboot, if a Droid) for **1d10 minutes** from systemic shock, regardless of the damage value.

---

## Wound Penalties in Play

Wound penalties apply to **every action** — attacking, dodging, slicing, piloting, everything.

<TWWrapper>
    <CharRoll attr="Dexterity" skill="Blaster" />
</TWWrapper>

If the character above is Wounded (-3), their effective pool drops by 3 dice. The roll button reflects the full pool — the player subtracts the penalty manually.

:::tip[Tracking Penalties]
The health bar on your character sheet at `/universal_sheet` updates the penalty automatically. Hover over any marked level to see the current penalty.
:::

## Stun Damage in Detail

Stun works differently from Bashing and Lethal:

1. **Recovers fast** — 1 level per turn. A character stunned at Injured recovers to full in 3 turns.
2. **Halts on new damage** — If a character takes Bashing or Lethal while recovering from Stun, quick recovery stops and the Stun becomes regular damage.
3. **Incapacitation** — If Stun fills the track to Incapacitated, the character falls unconscious for **1d10 minutes**. They wake at Bruised with all Stun cleared.
4. **Ion vs. Droids** — Blasters set to stun have no effect on droids. Ion weapons deal Stun damage to droids (and do nothing to living beings).

## Healing & Recovery

Natural healing times depend on damage type and medical care.

### Recovery Times

| Level         | Diff | Stun      | Bashing  | Lethal\*   |
| ------------- | ---- | --------- | -------- | ---------- |
| Bruised       | 4    | 1 turn    | 1 hour   | 1 day      |
| Hurt          | 5    | 1 turn    | 1 hour   | 3 days     |
| Injured       | 6    | 1 turn    | 1 hour   | 1 week     |
| Wounded       | 7    | 1 turn    | 1 hour   | 1 month\*  |
| Mauled        | 8    | 1 turn    | 3 hours  | 2 months\* |
| Crippled      | 9    | 1 turn    | 6 hours  | 3 months\* |
| Incapacitated | 10   | 1d10 mins | 12 hours | 5 months\* |

\* Requires stabilization or the character loses 1 Lethal health level per hour.

### Medical Attention

A character with **Intelligence + Medicine** can accelerate recovery. Roll with difficulty based on the patient's current health level. **Divide the recovery time by the number of successes** (minimum divisor of 1.5 for a single success).

<TWWrapper>
    <InlineRoll notation={'5d10\u003e=6f=1'} variant="details" preroll />
</TWWrapper>

**Equipment Bonuses:**

| Equipment        | Effect                                        |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| First Aid Medpac | Reduces difficulty by **1**                   |
| Infirmary        | Reduces difficulty by **2**                   |
| Hospital         | Reduces difficulty by **3**                   |
| Bacta Tank       | Converts recovery time from **days to hours** |

**Botch on Medicine:** The patient immediately loses **1 health level**. This can kill an Incapacitated character.

### Stabilization

Characters at **Wounded or below** from Lethal damage require stabilization or they lose **1 additional health level per hour**.

Roll **Intelligence + Medicine** or **Wits + Survival** at difficulty equal to the patient's current health level from the recovery table (modified by equipment) to stabilize. On success, the patient stops bleeding out and natural recovery can begin.

---

## Bacta Tanks

A bacta tank converts recovery from days to hours. A character with Lethal damage placed in a bacta tank under medical supervision can recover from Mauled to full in under 24 hours.

Bacta tanks require:

- A functioning medical facility (ship's medbay, Rebel base hospital)
- A character with Medicine to monitor the treatment
- 24 hours of uninterrupted immersion for full recovery from severe wounds

## Amputations

Amputations are a real threat in the Star Wars galaxy — lightsabers, explosions, and even blaster shots can sever limbs.

### Causing an Amputation

**Attacker's choice:** The attacker must target a specific limb (use the Targeting rules — +2 difficulty to hit). If the attack deals **3+ Lethal damage after soak**, the attacker may choose to amputate instead of dealing regular damage.

**Victim's choice:** If a character suffers Lethal damage after soaking, they may instead choose to **lose a body part** — the damage is avoided but the character immediately drops to **Incapacitated** (Bashing) from shock and gains an appropriate Flaw:

## Droid Damage & Recovery

<TWWrapper>
    <HealthViewer levels={['slash', 'slash', 'slash', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty', 'empty']} />
</TWWrapper>

Droid damage shown: three levels of ion/stun damage (Bruised, Hurt, Injured).

| Aspect                     | Droid Rule                                                                          |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Natural healing**        | None. Droids do not heal Bashing or Lethal naturally.                               |
| **De-ionization**          | Stun/ion damage recovers at the same rate as Stun for living beings (1 level/turn). |
| **Stabilization**          | Not needed. Droids never continue losing health levels.                             |
| **Environmental immunity** | Heat, cold, poison — no effect.                                                     |
| **Repair**                 | Uses vehicle repair rules (Permanent Repairs).                                      |

See [Droids & Cyborgs](../character/droids-cyborgs.mdx) for full droid repair rules.
