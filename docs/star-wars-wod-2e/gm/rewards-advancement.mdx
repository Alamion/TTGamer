# Rewards & Advancement

Characters grow through experience. This chapter covers how to award XP, what it costs to improve traits, and what the Storyteller should require before a dot is spent.

---

## Awarding Experience Points

At the end of each session, award **1–5 XP** per character. At the end of a story arc, award **1–3 bonus XP**.

| Criteria            | XP  | When to Award                                                                                                                |
| ------------------- | --- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Automatic**       | 1   | Every session. Just for showing up.                                                                                          |
| **Learning**        | 1   | The character learned something new. Ask the player to describe what.                                                        |
| **Acting**          | 1   | The player roleplayed well — entertaining and appropriate. Award to the best roleplayer of the session.                      |
| **Nature/Demeanor** | 1   | The player acted out their character's Nature and Demeanor convincingly.                                                     |
| **Heroism**         | 1   | The character put themselves at risk for others. Don't let players farm this — there's a line between heroism and stupidity. |
| **Success**         | 1   | The group achieved its goal (or made meaningful progress).                                                                   |
| **Danger**          | 1   | The character survived significant danger.                                                                                   |
| **Wisdom**          | 1   | A player's idea or insight turned the tide.                                                                                  |

### Pacing Guidelines

| Pace                           | XP per session | Traits improve to...       |
| ------------------------------ | -------------- | -------------------------- |
| **Slow** (year-long chronicle) | 2–3            | 4–5 dice after 20 sessions |
| **Moderate** (6-month arc)     | 4–5            | 5–6 dice after 20 sessions |
| **Fast** (movie-style trilogy) | 6–8            | 7+ dice after 20 sessions  |

A trait can only be raised **by one dot per story**. No buying from 2 to 5 in one go.

## Spending XP

### Standard Trait Costs

| Trait                                | Cost (XP)              |
| ------------------------------------ | ---------------------- |
| **Attribute**                        | current rating × 4     |
| **Ability** (Talent/Skill/Knowledge) | current rating × 2     |
| **New Ability**                      | 3                      |
| **Virtue**                           | current rating × 2     |
| **Willpower**                        | current rating         |
| **Background**                       | current rating × 2     |
| **Force Skill**                      | current rating × 2     |
| **New Force Power**                  | 2                      |
| **New Force Skill**                  | 3 (requires a teacher) |

_Example: Raising an Ability from 2 to 3 costs 2 × 2 = 4 XP. Raising an Attribute from 3 to 4 costs 3 × 4 = 12 XP._

### Dark Side Resistance

DSR uses a **reverse cost**: the lower your current rating, the more it costs to raise.

| Current DSR | Cost to raise by 1 |
| ----------- | ------------------ |
| 0           | 10 XP              |
| 1           | 9 XP               |
| 2           | 8 XP               |
| 3           | 7 XP               |
| 4           | 6 XP               |
| 5           | 5 XP               |
| 6           | 4 XP               |
| 7           | 3 XP               |
| 8           | 2 XP               |
| 9           | 1 XP               |

### Force Skills and Powers

When a Force user spends XP to **increase a Force Skill**, they also **learn one new Force Power** that relies on that skill — no extra cost. If they want a specific power tied to a skill they're not advancing, they pay **2 XP** to learn it separately.

## Training and Narrative Requirements

XP alone is not enough. The Storyteller should require:

- **Attributes** — The character used the Attribute heavily and pushed its limits. Strength gains require physical strain; Intelligence gains require study or problem-solving.
- **Abilities** — The character practiced or used the Ability in meaningful situations. A new Ability requires training (a teacher, a manual, or trial-and-error).
- **Virtues** — The character faced a moral test. Virtues cannot be raised in a vacuum.
- **Willpower** — Something must have happened to make the character more self-confident — surviving a trial, overcoming a fear, defying an authority.
- **Force Skills** — A teacher (holocron, Jedi Master, Sith spirit) or intense personal discovery. The Force does not yield to casual study.
- **Backgrounds** — Spending XP on a Background means the character earned it in play — a new ally made, a reputation gained, a resource acquired.
- **Humanity/DSR** — See [Dark Side Resistance](../character/dark_side_resistance.mdx).

The more you force the players to justify their advancement through story, the more immersive the game becomes.

## Freebie Points (Character Creation)

Character creation uses **Freebie Points** instead of XP. The costs differ.

| Trait                    | Freebie Cost                |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------- |
| **Attribute**            | 5 per dot                   |
| **Ability**              | 2 per dot                   |
| **Virtue**               | 2 per dot                   |
| **Background**           | 1 per dot                   |
| **Force Skill**          | 3 per dot                   |
| **Force Power**          | 2 per power                 |
| **Dark Side Resistance** | 1 per dot                   |
| **Willpower**            | 2 per dot                   |
| **Merit**                | varies (see Player's Guide) |

Starting Freebie Points: **15** (humans), adjusted by species. See [Species](../character/species.mdx) for non-human adjustments.

### Freebie vs. XP Comparison

| Trait           | Freebie Cost | XP Cost     |
| --------------- | ------------ | ----------- |
| Attribute dot   | 5            | current × 4 |
| Ability dot     | 2            | current × 2 |
| Virtue dot      | 2            | current × 2 |
| Willpower dot   | 2            | current     |
| Background dot  | 1            | current × 2 |
| Force Skill dot | 3            | current × 2 |
| Force Power     | 2            | 2           |

## Droid Advancement

Droids do not spend XP on Attributes or Abilities the same way living characters do:

- **Attributes** are upgraded like vehicle systems. They require credits, parts, and a Tech roll (not XP). See [Modifications](../vehicles-mechanisms/modifications.mdx).
- **Abilities (programming)** can be upgraded with XP normally, or reprogrammed during downtime with a Tech (Computers) roll.
- **New Abilities** require finding compatible programming — either purchased, copied from another droid, or written from scratch.
